Hi mate, I went to the doctors a few weeks ago with this/shin splints - I wasn’t sure which! My GP referred me to a local nhs physio for a phone call. They gave me 2 calf exercises to do once per day, single leg heel raises (fast on the way up then 6 seconds on the way down) and toe taps. Calf stretches/foam rolling to do 3 times per day. They said do this for 4 weeks then return to running with a few minutes running/few minutes walking routine. Should be back to 100% after 12 weeks apparently.

Maybe tailor the exercises to the posterior tibialis in your case?

I think mine’s caused by low arches and my ankles over pronating, but they couldn’t confirm this without seeing me in person. I can’t imagine me being a bit overweight helps the issue! I was expecting to get orthotic insoles but they said it’s best to do exercises like this first, rather than constrict your natural movement with insoles.

Hopefully not a bar to entry and will just be asked for a 3 month exercise diary

Rest, do non-impact cardio if there is no pain during movement (cycling, rowing, stairs machine) and get on this programme!

KNEE PREHAB

Save your knees with Ben Patrick. Knee Ability's Stretching programme https://youtube.com/playlist?list=PLiNqNFYPXm_JAv0oFPAIMYuc8UyUIiyzF Knee's Ability's Zero programme https://youtube.com/playlist?list=PLiNqNFYPXm_L3oi5c743lVSMLTqhYN_E8
www.royalmarines.uk

It helps with shin splints as well!

If you spoke with your GP about it, it will be on your medical records so from today start writing a training journey.

Read this, don't want to copy-paste the whole thing:

Shin splints

Anyone have any advice on how to get rid of shin splints , and how to prevent getting them in the future ?
www.paras.uk

Speak to your therapists about implementing the below programme:
Give it at least 2-3 months to heal with these prehab exercises (done 3 times a week and involves backwards walking as well if no pain), then follow the Ohio State University's BASIC WALK TO RUN PROGRESSION for 4 weeks than the Naval Special Warfare's PT guide for the run progression IF THERE IS NO MORE PAIN.
To be safe it is going to be a 3-4 months journey before you start running longer distances and doing intervals again but by taking the time you will let the tissues heal. 4 months is nothing in your lifetime. In the meantime focus on non-impact cardio, core and upper body strength and lower body strength IF there is no pain.

You will also be TMU'd once at that stage so collect evidence that you are doing these exercises and once running again have GPS data to support it.

You can thank @Vine for the basic walk to run progression.
