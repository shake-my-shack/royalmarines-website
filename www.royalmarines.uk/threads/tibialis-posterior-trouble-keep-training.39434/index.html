Evening lads.
I've been having trouble with weak lower legs recently and an injury of what I thought was my Achilles Tendon but on further investigation it's looking more like the Tibialis Posterior that's giving me trouble.

The source of the pain is just behind the ankle where the tendon meets the muscle but it's causing pain in the whole rear inside of my foot to the top right of my ankle.

I've been blasting ice on it and doing strengthening exercises which have reduced pain significantly in just one session!

Would I be able to keep running while doing these strengthening exercises while taking Anti-Inflammatories to tackle any inflammation or do I need to rest completely?

I'm going to book a an appointment with my GP and see what they say about it because I need a line to hand in to the AFCO. Putting off your app about PJFT time is rather hard.

Sorry for the essay.

Hi mate,

Just to say how i have prevented my shin splints from returning.

I had some pain in the posterior region once but i rested up and stopped running for a while.

I prevent them from coming back by taking the time to stretch my calfs out after a session. I hold a 30 second stretch on each calf then repeat.

I also do a light achilles stretch, which is where you kind of sit down into it ( just go easy on these ones though)!

Also do squats like on the RM website, make sure you keep your heels on the floor. I was absolutely useless at these. I could feel alot of pressure in my lower legs as I did them, but after practising them the pressure has almost dissapeered.

Good luck mate

P.s I can't even remember what the original thread question was haha, but hope that helps anyway!

On the squats try to get lower down each time,
